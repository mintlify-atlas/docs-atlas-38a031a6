This guide walks you through the complete nutritionist workflow, from setting up your schedule to managing patients and conducting consultations.

## Dashboard Overview

Your dashboard shows:

- **Appointments Today**: Number of pending appointments for today
- **Appointments This Week**: Week's scheduled consultations
- **Next Appointment**: Your upcoming consultation with patient details
- **Today's Schedule**: Complete agenda with appointment times
- **Upcoming Schedule**: Next 4 weeks of appointments organized by week

<Note>
Expired appointments (past their end time without completion) are automatically marked as "expired" and removed from your pending list.
</Note>

## Setting Up Your Schedule

### Initial Schedule Configuration

Before accepting appointments, configure your availability:

1. Navigate to **Schedules** from your dashboard
2. You'll see a weekly grid with time slots from 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM
3. Click on time slots to mark your availability
4. Select multiple consecutive slots to create availability blocks
5. Click **Save Schedule** when finished

### Schedule Details

- Each appointment slot is **45 minutes** (standard consultation duration)
- You can set availability for any day of the week
- Multiple time blocks can be created for the same day (e.g., morning and afternoon sessions)
- Time slots are displayed in 45-minute intervals

### Editing Your Schedule

<Warning>
When you save a new schedule, all previous schedule settings are deleted and replaced with the new configuration.
</Warning>

To modify your schedule:

1. Go to **Schedules**
2. Select new availability slots
3. Previously selected slots are shown for reference
4. Click **Save Schedule**
5. Confirm the changes

### Schedule Considerations

- Existing pending appointments are not affected by schedule changes
- Only future appointment slots are impacted
- Patients cannot book slots that conflict with existing appointments
- Your schedule is automatically shown to patients in their timezone

## Managing Patients

### Patient List

Access your patient roster:

1. Click **Patients** in the navigation
2. View all patients who have had appointments with you
3. Search by name or email
4. Filter by appointment status

### Patient Details

Click any patient to view:

- **Personal Information**: Name, email, age, gender
- **Contact Details**: Phone and emergency contact
- **Appointment History**: All past and upcoming appointments
- **Statistics**:
  - Total appointments
  - Completed sessions
  - Cancelled appointments
  - Pending appointments
- **Next Appointment**: Upcoming consultation details
- **Last Attention**: Most recent clinical record

### Viewing Patient Data

Access comprehensive patient information:

1. Open patient profile
2. Click **View Patient Data**
3. Review:
   - Personal demographics
   - Medical history
   - Previous measurements
   - Progress photos (if available)

### Patient History

Track patient progress over time:

1. Open patient profile
2. Click **View History**
3. See interactive charts showing:
   - Weight progression
   - BMI changes
   - Body fat percentage
   - Circumference measurements (waist, hip, arms, legs)
   - Metabolic rates (TMB, TDEE)
   - Body ratios (WHR, WHT)
   - Target calories over time

<Tip>
Use the history charts during consultations to show patients their progress visually.
</Tip>

## Appointment Management

### Viewing Your Appointments

Access all appointments:

1. Click **Appointments** in navigation
2. View appointments with filters:
   - **All**: Every appointment regardless of status
   - **Pending**: Upcoming scheduled appointments
   - **Completed**: Finished consultations with records
   - **Cancelled**: Cancelled by you or patient
   - **Expired**: Past appointments not completed
3. Search by patient name
4. Filter by date range

### Creating Appointments for Patients

You can schedule appointments on behalf of patients:

1. Click **Assign Appointment**
2. Select patient from dropdown (searchable)
3. System shows patient information and checks:
   - Patient must not have existing pending appointments
   - Patient must be clinically eligible (active account)
4. Select available date and time from calendar
5. Choose appointment type:
   - **First Visit** (primera_vez): Initial consultation
   - **Follow-up** (seguimiento): Regular follow-up session
   - **Control** (control): Check-up or monitoring session
6. Enter consultation reason (optional)
7. Add internal notes (optional)
8. Set price (defaults to your consultation rate)
9. Click **Create Appointment**

Both you and the patient receive email confirmations.

### Appointment Calendar View

The calendar displays:

- 4 weeks of availability starting from current week
- Only days with available time slots
- Slots marked as unavailable if already booked
- Time displayed in 12-hour format (e.g., 9:00 AM)

### Appointment Details

Click any appointment to view:

- Patient information and contact
- Scheduled date and time
- Appointment type and reason
- Price
- Status (pending, completed, etc.)
- Clinical notes (if completed)
- Medical measurements (if recorded)

### Rescheduling Appointments

To reschedule a pending appointment:

1. Open the appointment details
2. Click **Reschedule Appointment**
3. Select new date and time from available slots
4. Optionally add reason for rescheduling
5. Click **Confirm Reschedule**

The patient receives an automatic notification with:
- Original appointment time
- New appointment time
- Reason for change (if provided)

<Note>
Only pending appointments can be rescheduled. Completed, cancelled, or expired appointments cannot be modified.
</Note>

### Cancelling Appointments

To cancel an appointment:

1. Open the appointment details
2. Click **Cancel Appointment**
3. Confirm the cancellation

Automatic notifications are sent:
- You receive immediate confirmation
- Patient receives notification (with 20-second delay for development environments)

<Warning>
Only pending appointments can be cancelled. Consider rescheduling instead if possible.
</Warning>

## Conducting Consultations

### Starting a Consultation

1. From dashboard or appointment list, click on the appointment
2. Click **Start Attention** button
3. You'll be taken to the clinical data entry form

### Recording Clinical Data

During the consultation, record:

**Basic Measurements**:
- Weight (kg)
- Height (cm)
- BMI (auto-calculated)

**Circumference Measurements**:
- Waist (cm)
- Hip (cm)
- Neck (cm)
- Wrist (cm)
- Arm contracted (cm)
- Arm relaxed (cm)
- Thigh (cm)
- Calf (cm)

**Calculated Metrics**:
- Body fat percentage
- TMB (Basal Metabolic Rate)
- TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)
- WHR (Waist-to-Hip Ratio)
- WHT (Waist-to-Height Ratio)
- Target calories

**Clinical Information**:
- Medical history notes
- Current symptoms or concerns
- Dietary habits
- Physical activity level
- Sleep patterns
- Goals and objectives

**Nutrition Plan**:
- Meal recommendations
- Portion guidelines
- Foods to include/avoid
- Supplement recommendations
- Hydration requirements

**Follow-up Notes**:
- Progress assessment
- Next steps
- Homework or assignments
- Recommended follow-up date

### Saving Clinical Records

1. Fill in all required fields
2. Review data for accuracy
3. Click **Save Attention**
4. Appointment automatically changes to "completed" status

### Editing Clinical Records

If you need to modify a record:

1. Open the completed appointment
2. Click **Edit Attention**
3. Update the information
4. Click **Update** to save changes

<Tip>
Save records immediately after consultation to ensure data accuracy and patient safety.
</Tip>

### Generating PDF Reports

Create professional reports for patients:

1. Open completed appointment
2. Click **Download PDF** or **View PDF**
3. PDF includes:
   - Patient information
   - Consultation date
   - All measurements and metrics
   - Clinical notes
   - Nutrition plan
   - Your signature and credentials

## Schedule Conflicts

The system automatically prevents:

- Double-booking: Cannot book two appointments at same time
- Past bookings: Cannot schedule appointments in the past
- Overlapping slots: 45-minute slots cannot overlap

### Resolving Conflicts

If a patient reports a conflict:

1. Check your appointment list for the time slot
2. Verify the patient's appointment status
3. Reschedule one appointment if needed
4. Contact the patient to confirm new time

## Best Practices

### Schedule Management

- Update your schedule at least one week in advance
- Block out vacation time immediately
- Keep consistent weekly hours for patient convenience
- Leave buffer time between appointments for notes

### Patient Communication

- Review patient history before appointments
- Send reminders for upcoming consultations
- Follow up on missed appointments
- Document all communication in notes

### Clinical Records

- Complete records immediately after consultation
- Be thorough with measurements and observations
- Set clear, measurable goals with patients
- Document progress and setbacks objectively

### Time Management

- Start appointments on time
- Keep consultations within 45-minute window
- Schedule administrative tasks between appointments
- Review next day's appointments each evening

## Workflow Summary

1. **Set up your schedule** with available time slots
2. **Receive appointment bookings** from patients or create them yourself
3. **Review patient information** before consultations
4. **Conduct consultation** and record clinical data
5. **Save attention records** to complete the appointment
6. **Generate PDF reports** for patient records
7. **Track patient progress** through history charts
8. **Schedule follow-ups** as needed

<Tip>
Establish a consistent routine for reviewing appointments, updating schedules, and following up with patients.
</Tip>
